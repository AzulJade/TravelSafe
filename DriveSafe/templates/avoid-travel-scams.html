Avoiding Common Travel Scams

Stay alert to common travel scams that target tourists and travelers. Here’s how to avoid being tricked:

Agree on the Fare

Always agree on the fare before starting your journey if the taxi does not use a meter.

Be Wary of Destination Changes

Be wary of taxi drivers who suggest changing your destination to a “better” or “safer” place.

Avoid Too-Good-To-Be-True Prices

Avoid drivers who offer too-good-to-be-true prices or ask for upfront payments.

Use Official Taxi Stands

Use official taxi stands or apps, and avoid accepting rides from random drivers at airports or stations.

Additional Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

Research common scams in the area you are visiting.

Trust your instincts; if something feels off, seek help or leave the situation.

Keep your belongings secure and be cautious of distractions.
